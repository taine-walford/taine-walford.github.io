28 April 19

Long term goal:

Leave something behind. Specifically a story of my own, but my career is simply something that challenges me and something that I can take to the highest level.

What human skills will you develop at EDA:

- Critical mind: I don't struggle with being open with opinions and with making leader decisions, however I need to learn to evaluate better and make better decisions. I find too often that I say something without any critical thought behind it.
- Calm: I struggle with taking a step back and not being lost in the moment, which I will be working on. I love the rush of the moment and getting insane focus on something, but sometimes that isn't what I should be focused on.
- Time management: Time management in both macro and micro sense; Making sure that I'm using my time wisely in the long run but also in the moment.

Strengths:

- Confident in myself and quick to throw out ideas
- Good problem solving skills
- Passionate about a lot of things
- Open with people and glad to make new friends

Limitations:

- Struggle with studying/learning
- Awful at time management
- Problem solving skillset leans toward immediate solutions over a better solution

Workload management:

Due to working the weekends, Monday has been my relaxing day with Tues- Sat afternoon being my work-week.
In this time I need to divvy up the workload into what needs to be completed, and then into what days I do what, and then give myself hard deadlines that I need to meet.
This will help with my time management and hone my mind to remain calm and focused on the bigger picture.
Aside from working, I have no commitments at the moment, meaning I can focus a large chunk of my time into my learning plan. I aim to spend 5ish hours on campus per day, to allow for some socializing before Bootcamp starts. This will open up conversation, allow new situations, and I can seek help quickly and with multiple people.
This will allow me to push my critical mind and allow for maximum change in my mind.
Due to the nature of a flipped classroom, I except Chris to take a spectator role and oversee my learning by checking in with me. If he has to seek me out, obviously I'm not doing well.
